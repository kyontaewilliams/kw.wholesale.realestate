Already Lost Your Home?

You May Have Money Waiting for You

If your home was sold at a foreclosure auction in California, this could apply to you.

What are surplus funds?
When a foreclosed home sells at auction for more than the amount owed to the lender, the extra money is called surplus funds. By California law, that money belongs to the former homeowner — not the bank, not the investor who bought the property.
Why most people never collect
The money doesn't come to you automatically. It sits with the county or court waiting to be claimed. Most former homeowners don't know it exists — and there's a deadline to claim it before the funds are turned over to the state.
